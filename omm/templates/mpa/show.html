Economic Impact Analyses

These reports display estimates of the potential maximum economic impact of your Area of Interest to certain human use activities and include estimates of gross economic revenue (GER), estimated economic output (EEO), and estimated personal income (EPI) for certain sectors.

  • Com (NOAA) reports GER and EEO from commercial fishing at the harvesting level. These estimates use economic multipliers provided by NOAA for the state of Oregon.

  • Com (FEAM) reports GER and EPI from commercial fishing harvesting and processing activities. These estimates are derived from the Fisheries Economic Assessment Model (FEAM) produced by The Research Group (2005).

  • Charter reports GER and EEO from charter fishing. These estimates use the economic multiplier provided by Gentner and Steinback (2008).

  • Rec reports EPI from recreational fishing trips.

A full report on the methods and multipliers used in these analyses are forthcoming. A link to that report will be added here as soon as it becomes available.

Learn More

The estimated economic output (EEO) resulting from an economic activity is measured by applying economic multipliers to estimated GERs. The purpose of applying multipliers is to capture the ripple effects of economic activity; simply, a direct change in one industry affects other industries. For example, a multiplier of 1.5 implies that every $1.00 of GER in a sector, i.e. commercial fishing, generates an additional $0.50 in economic activity throughout the economy, for a total economic output of $1.50. Estimated personal income (EPI) is a measure of the portion of direct and indirect economic activity associated with sales or expenditures in a certain sector that accrues to households in terms of wages, salaries, and profits.
